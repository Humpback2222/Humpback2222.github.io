Dense Contact Barrier Functions for Safe Robot Interaction

Manipulation, Research Experience, May 2025 – Sept 2025

Haixin Jin, Nikhil Shinde, Soofiyan Atar, Hongzhan Yu, Dylan Hirsch, Sicun Gao, Michael C. Yip, Sylvia Herbert "Learning to Nudge: A Scalable Barrier Function Framework for Safe Robot Interaction in Dense Clutter" Submitted to IEEE International Conference on Robotics and Automation (ICRA) 2026

We propose a novel Dense Contact Barrier Function (DCBF) framework enabling robots to safely interact with and navigate densely cluttered environments. Designed and implemented an object-centric neural barrier model that implicitly encodes inter-object contact dynamics through history-conditioned representations, supporting scalable and generalizable safety reasoning. Validated the framework across environments with up to 40 objects, demonstrating strong transfer and robustness in dense multi-object scenarios despite training solely on 4-object settings.
